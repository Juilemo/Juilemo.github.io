ANALYSIS OF THE PASSAGE

The passage explains why some people who learned Spanish in the US may have difficulty understanding some native speakers. It says that Spanish is a very diverse language, with different dialects in different regions and countries. It contrasts the standard Latin American Spanish, which is taught in most US schools, with the Castilian Spanish, which is spoken in parts of Spain. It highlights some of the main differences between these two dialects, such as the pronunciation of certain letters and the use of pronouns. It also mentions the historical origins of these dialects and how they relate to each other.

  1. This does not ( 1 ) mean the lessons were wasted.
    First, let’s go through the chooses, which are all adverbs.

  • necessarily, of course, is adverb from necessary, which means a necessary result.
    synonyms: inevitably

  • usually is a adverb to express the frequency, which can be understood as under normal conditions or generally.

  • particularly means more than normal, whose synonym is “especially“.

    In this sentence, the author wants to express that the lessons those people have learned were not wasted. Here is a double negative. Double negatives cancel one another and produce an affirmative, especially in Modern English, Chinese, Japanese, etc.

  1. It is sometimes called “Highland” Spanish since it is generally spoken in the ( 2 ) areas of Latin America.
    This is a synonymous substitution question. “Highland“ literally means a high land. “Mountainous“ means a place having many mountains.

The differences between “characteristic“ and “distinguish“
To start, both words can be used to describe unique qualities or features of something. However, “distinguishing” refers to qualities that set something apart from others, while “characteristic” refers to qualities that are typical or common to something.

For example, if you were describing a new car model, you might say that its distinguishing features are its sleek design and advanced technology. These are qualities that make the car stand out from other models on the market. On the other hand, you might say that the car’s characteristic features are its fuel efficiency and spacious interior. These are qualities that are common to many cars in the same class.

Understanding the difference between these two terms can help you more effectively communicate your thoughts and ideas. Throughout the rest of this article, we’ll explore the nuances of distinguishing vs characteristic in more detail, and provide examples of how to use each term correctly.

Define Distinguishing

Distinguishing refers to the act of differentiating or separating something from others based on unique qualities, features, or characteristics. It is the process of identifying what sets something apart from the rest and making it stand out. For instance, in the business world, companies use distinguishing factors to set themselves apart from their competitors and attract customers. These distinguishing factors could be in the form of unique product features, superior customer service, or innovative marketing strategies.

Furthermore, distinguishing can also refer to the ability to recognize or identify something or someone based on specific traits or characteristics. In this context, distinguishing is an important skill that is useful in various fields, such as law enforcement, medicine, and science. For example, a forensic scientist may use distinguishing features such as fingerprints, DNA, or dental records to identify a suspect or victim.

Define Characteristic

A characteristic is a distinguishing feature or quality that is typical or inherent in something or someone. It is a defining trait that helps to identify or describe something. Characteristics can be physical, behavioral, or psychological in nature and can be used to differentiate between similar things or individuals. For example, the characteristic features of a lion include a mane, sharp teeth, and a powerful roar.

In addition, characteristics can also refer to the qualities or attributes that are associated with a particular group or category. These characteristics could be cultural, social, or economic in nature. For instance, the characteristics of a successful entrepreneur may include traits such as risk-taking, innovation, and perseverance.
